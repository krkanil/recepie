Dumpling (MoMo) Recipe

History about Dumplings

Most experts believe that dumplings were invented by Zhang Zhongjing, a Chinese medicine practitioner who lived in the Eastern Han Dynasty, the second imperial dynasty of China that lasted from 206 BC to 220 AD. While the history of dumplings is often accepted as having started with Zhang Zhongjing, the general idea of dumplings as we know them today followed quite an exciting and varied trajectory.

Mo:Mo is an integral part of every Nepalese out there. Momo (Nepali dumpling) has become the perfect fast-food item for all age groups within Nepal, and it would not be wrong to call it “the king of fast food. “The reason being its taste and availability. Moreover, these dumplings can be developed in a lot of its variety which including Fried, Steam, Jhol, Chilly, Sandeko, Open, Kothey, Tandoori, and many more. Since the filling can be adjusted according to our taste buds, It is almost impossible to be not liked. Momo usually is preferred as an afternoon snack although it is consumed for lunch and dinner. Momo made of buff, chicken meat or veg is more common in Nepal. To prepare Momo is quite easy: What we need is flour, salt, baking powder (optional) with oil, onion, and garlic added to taste. Momo is cooked by direct steam heat and is served with delicious spices or chutney. Momo comes in both round and elongated shapes and a plate consists of 10 momo dumplings. It is undoubtedly one of the cheapest and tastiest foods available in Nepal. Momo is a dish found in every city, town, and village in Nepal, from local restaurants to five-star hotels. Everest Momo is one of the famous outlets for momo lovers for its typical ingredients and preparation.

Required Ingredients:

Dough for Wrappers

  • 4 Cups All-purpose flour
  • 1 Teaspoon oil
  • 1 pinch salt
  • Water as required to make dough

Filling for Dumpling

  • 2 lbs lean ground meat
  • 1 cup chopped mushroom
  • 1 cup chopped red onion
  • 1 cup chopped cabbage
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh garlic
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger
  • 2 tablespoon coriander and cumin powder
  • 2 tablespoon dumpling spices
  • 1 tablespoon redchiili power
  • 1 tablespoon melted butter
  • 1 tablespoon salt

Varieties of cooked MoMo

  • Steamed MoMo:

    • These small dumpling are then kept in the steamer and fully cooked approx 15 minutes after the water is boiled. These momos are always healthier than the other types as there is no extra oil or fat added during cooking process.

  • Fried MoMo:

    • Fried Momos are another famous type of momos where the dumpling is deep fried. Oil is placed in the frying utensils and steamed momos are deep fried inside it until it changes its color to brown. These crunchy version of momos are fried after steaming the momos.

  • Jhol MoMo:

    • Jhol momos basically consists of liquid or having a liquid-like consistency. The taste of jhol momo depends on the taste of jhol(liquid sauce) prepared. The dumplings in Jhol momos are drowned in a bowl full of sauce called jhol achar.

  • Kothey MoMo:

    • Kothey momos are best option which has the best of both steam dumplings and pan fried which has a crusty skin. As in this type one side of momo is fried and one is left as it is after steamed.

  • Chilli MoMo:

    • Chilly momos are the dumplings dipped in a hot and spicy sauce. It is most commonly known as C. Momo. These are very hot and spicy type as it contains a good amount sauce that is made of chilli paste, onion, capsicum, tomato and other hot spices.

Preparation: Steps to Prepare Dumpling

Step 1:

Prepare the Wrappers

  1. Make a loop / rope with the dough.
  2. Cut it into equal sections.
  3. Press each piece into a small disc with the palm of your hand.
  4. Use a rolling pin to flatten it into a thin disc.

Step 2:

Prepare the Fillings

  1. Put all the filling ingredents in the big bowl
  2. Use hand to mix all the ingredents to spread the spices evenly
  3. Let the filling sit for 20 minutes to marinate either in room temperature or in the fridge.

Step 3:

Folding the Dumplings

  1. Take a piece of dumpling wrapper
  2. Scoop about a spoonful of filling to the center of the wrapper.
  3. Dip your fingers into the water and draw along the edge of the wrapper.
  4. Now fold the wrapper in half and pinch the top edge.
  5. Hold the dumplings between the index finger and thumb of both hands.
  6. Make a pleat on each side with your index fingers.
  7. Squeeze with your thumbs and index fingers to seal.
  8. Repeat above 2 steps few more times.
  9. Give the edge a pinch to seal.
  10. Place the finished dumplings on steamer utensils.

Step 4:

Cooking the Dumplings

  1. Prepare a pot of water to boil.
  2. Place the steamer utensils on top of boling water.
  3. Let the steamer be on top for 15 mins.
  4. Remove the dumplings from the steamer.

Step 5:

Serving/ Enjoying the Dumplings

  1. Make a tomato mix chatni.
  2. Server the dumpling in a plate.
  3. Place the chatni in small bowl and place it in the center of the plate.
  4. Dip the dumpling in the chatni and Enjoy!!
